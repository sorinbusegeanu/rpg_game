# Sea of Broken Oaths

## Core premise

The age of heroes is ending. The gods have withdrawn from the world, but their old oaths, curses, monsters, and divine mechanisms still remain.

The player leads a new band of Argonauts sent to recover the Golden Fleece, not as treasure, but as the last divine relic capable of sealing a spreading corruption called the Black Wind.

The journey is not only across the sea. Each island is a dungeon-region with ancient trials, monsters, local rulers, and moral choices.

```text
Ship / Base -> Island Exploration -> Encounter -> Combat -> Relic / Reward -> Upgrade Heroes -> Sail Further
```

## Why this story fits the game

Sea of Broken Oaths uses the public-domain structure of classical Greek myth, especially the Argonauts and the quest for the Golden Fleece, but presents it as an original dark-fantasy RPG campaign.

The structure naturally supports:

- a team of heroes
- a ship as the upgrade hub
- island-by-island exploration
- mythic monsters
- relic gear
- divine curses
- boss encounters
- long-term campaign progression

## Main story arc

### Act 1 — The Broken Oath

The old kingdoms are collapsing. Monsters once bound by divine law now roam free. A black storm spreads from the east, turning forests, temples, coasts, and cities into cursed zones.

An oracle says only the Golden Fleece can restore the broken oath between mortals and the vanished gods.

The player begins with a small crew and the ship Argo, damaged but sacred.

### Act 2 — The Sea of Trials

The crew sails through mythic regions, each serving as a campaign chapter or dungeon zone.

#### The Whispering Coast

The opening region. A forested shore of ruined shrines, raiders, wolves, and cursed soldiers. This area introduces exploration, encounters, party combat, and the return-to-ship loop.

#### The Isle of Harpies

A starving island ruled by storm-winds and winged monsters. The crew must rescue a blind prophet tormented by harpies.

#### The Clashing Rocks

A dangerous passage of moving stone, wrecked ships, timed hazards, and ambushes. This region emphasizes survival, movement hazards, and environmental pressure.

#### The Bronze Island

A dead island guarded by bronze soldiers and the giant Talos, an ancient divine construct animated by sacred fire.

#### The Siren Reefs

A region of charm, silence, confusion, and false visions. Sirens and drowned sailors attack the mind before the body.

#### The Dead King’s Marsh

A poisonous marsh of drowned temples, skeleton warriors, plague beasts, and forgotten royal tombs.

#### Colchis

The final eastern kingdom. It is guarded by serpent warriors, fire bulls, oath-bound soldiers, and the dragon that watches the Golden Fleece.

### Act 3 — The Fleece Is Not Enough

The crew reaches the Golden Fleece, but the relic is corrupted by the same force it was meant to seal.

The final choice is not simply whether to take it, but what to do with it:

```text
Use the Fleece to save one kingdom.
Destroy it to break divine control.
Bind it to the Argo and continue the fight.
```

This keeps the world open for later campaigns, seasons, or expansions.

## Player fantasy

The player commands a mythic crew sailing through a broken ancient world where divine law has failed. The ship is home, base, and symbol of survival. Every expedition risks the crew, but every victory brings relics, gold, experience, and fragments of the truth.

The fantasy is not only to become stronger, but to rebuild trust after the gods abandoned their oaths.

## Core loop integration

```text
Exploration:
The party moves through island paths, forests, ruins, shores, marshes, and sacred sites.

Transition:
An omen appears. The screen darkens. The encounter begins.

Combat:
Card-style heartbeat combat against mythic enemies.

Rewards:
Gold, XP, relic shards, divine bronze, monster teeth, oracle fragments.

Base:
Return to the Argo to upgrade heroes, repair the ship, craft relic gear, and choose the next destination.

Progression:
Unlock new islands, recruit heroes, defeat bosses, and pursue the Golden Fleece.
```

## Party concept

The crew should support a team RPG structure with 3–5 active heroes and a larger roster over time.

Example hero archetypes:

| Hero archetype | Role | Gameplay function |
|---|---|---|
| Aeson’s Heir | Leader / Fighter | Balanced sword fighter, rally buffs |
| Medea the Veiled | Sorceress | Poison, curses, fire, control |
| Orion’s Daughter | Archer | Ranged damage, traps, anti-flying attacks |
| Boreas-Touched Twin | Rogue / Wind | Speed, evasion, anti-flying pressure |
| Bronze-Shield Ajax | Tank | Guard, taunt, shield wall |
| Orphean Singer | Support | Healing, morale, charm resistance |
| Heraklean Wanderer | Bruiser | Heavy burst damage, monster slayer |
| Oracle of Dodona | Priest / Seer | Prediction, cleanse, revive |

The names can remain myth-inspired without directly depending on one exact classical cast.

## Tone

Dark mythic fantasy. Ancient, tragic, dangerous, and mysterious.

The gods are absent, but their broken systems still shape the world. The sea is both a road and a threat. Every island is a test of loyalty, survival, and interpretation of old divine laws.
